Limestone County, AL April 2026

Project Overview

This month we cleared out a 200-yard shooting range right here in Limestone County. The customer wanted a long, clean shooting lane carved out of their property — so we brought in the equipment and got to work knocking down the brush, pulling stumps, and opening up the line of sight all the way down to 200 yards.

To finish it off, we pushed up a solid pile of dirt at the far end to act as a backstop berm. Having a good dirt pile to shoot into is a must on any range — it stops the rounds safely and keeps everything contained on the property.

End to end, the customer went from an overgrown patch of land to a wide-open, ready-to-use 200-yard range with a proper backstop in place.

What We Did

  • Cleared 200-yard shooting lane
  • Brush, tree, and stump removal
  • Opened up clean line of sight
  • Built backstop berm at the far end
  • Final cleanup and grading
